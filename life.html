Musical Training
Zakir began formal training in tabla at the age of 3 under the guidance of his father. By the age of 7, he was performing in public concerts. His rigorous training focused on both the technique and the deeper philosophy of music. Zakir's early years were marked by discipline, as he balanced academics and intense practice sessions.

Education
Zakir attended St. Michael's High School in Mumbai and later St. Xavier's College, where he earned a degree in Economics. Despite his academic pursuits, music remained his primary passion and focus.

Professional Career
Indian Classical Music Zakir Hussain is considered a leading exponent of the Punjab Gharana of tabla playing. He has performed as a solo artist and accompanied almost every major Hindustani classical artist, including Pandit Ravi Shankar, Ustad Vilayat Khan, and Pandit Shivkumar Sharma.

Music and Fusion
Shakti (1974): Zakir co-founded this iconic fusion band with John McLaughlin (guitar) and L. Shankar (violin). Collaborated with Western musicians such as George Harrison, Mickey Hart, and Yo-Yo Ma. His groundbreaking work in blending Indian classical music with jazz, rock, and world music made him a global icon.

Film Music and Compositions
Composed soundtracks for films, including: In Custody (directed by Ismail Merchant) Vanaprastham (won the National Film Award) Collaborated on Little Buddha (with Japanese composer Ryuichi Sakamoto) and Heat and Dust. Featured in The Speaking Hand, a documentary on the tabla.

Collaborations and Notable Albums
Planet Drum: Collaborated with Grateful Dead drummer Mickey Hart, winning the first-ever Grammy Award for Best World Music Album. Remember Shakti: A revived version of the original Shakti group that continued to tour globally. Albums include Making Music, Tabla Duet, Night Spinner, and Golden Strings of the Sarode.
